How to Pair Pastries with Kona Coffee

Home › Blog
By Mele AkanaMay 5, 20254 min read

Why Pairing Matters

Coffee and pastry are individually excellent. Together, the right combination creates something greater than either alone — the coffee elevates the pastry and the pastry transforms the coffee. This is classical cafÉ culture, and Kona's exceptional single-origin coffee offers some extraordinary pairing opportunities.

Understanding Kona Coffee's Flavor Profile

Kona coffee from our partner farms typically has: low acidity, smooth body, bright initial notes, and a clean finish with subtle chocolate, macadamia, and caramel undertones. This profile pairs well with rich, buttery, and not overly sweet pastries.

Our Favorite Pairings from the Lehua Menu

Kona Pour-Over + Plain Butter Croissant

The classic pairing. The coffee's brightness cuts through the buttery richness of the croissant perfectly. The clean finish of the Kona complements the flaky, laminated pastry without overpowering it. This is the pairing you want on a quiet Tuesday morning with a newspaper.

Iced Kona Latte + Kona Coffee Scone

Coffee on coffee sounds redundant but actually doubles down beautifully. The espresso concentrate in the latte amplifies the roasted notes in the scone, while the milk smooths everything out. The espresso glaze on the scone and the coffee in your cup create a unified taste experience.

Cold Brew + Macadamia Nut Muffin

Cold brew is lower acidity and slightly sweeter than hot coffee — perfect with the natural butteriness of macadamia nuts. The long cold-extraction process in our cold brew creates a chocolate-forward flavor that plays beautifully with the muffin's nut and brown sugar notes.

Kona Drip + Haupia Malasada

The coconut custard filling of a haupia malasada creates a tropical counterpoint to the volcanic earthiness of Kona drip coffee. The sugar coating on the malasada brings out sweetness in the coffee that you wouldn't otherwise notice. Bite, sip, repeat. This is what Saturday mornings are for.

The Rule of Thumb

When in doubt: rich pastry with strong coffee, light pastry with delicate coffee. Never pair a subtly flavored scone with a dark, intense espresso — the coffee will steamroll the pastry. Trust your palate, and don't rush.
