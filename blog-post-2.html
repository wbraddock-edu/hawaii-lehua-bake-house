Best Bakeries in Kailua-Kona: A Local's List

Kona's Underrated Baking Scene

Most visitors to the Big Island are focused on the volcanoes, the black sand beaches, and the manta rays — and rightfully so. But Kailua-Kona has quietly developed an impressive food scene, and its bakeries deserve serious attention. Here's an honest local's guide to the best baked goods in town.

For Traditional Hawaiian Pastry

Honalo Cafe, tucked into the old coffee town of Honalo about 10 minutes south of Kailua-Kona on the Mamalahoa Highway, is a local institution. Their malasadas are done the traditional way — no frills, just great dough and hot oil. The plate lunches here are also exceptional. It's a real neighborhood spot that feels unchanged since the 1970s.

For Japanese-Influenced Baked Goods

Kona's significant Japanese-American community has left a delicious culinary legacy. Look for mochi, manju (sweet bean paste pastries), and Japanese-style cream puffs at several local markets and small shops along Ali'i Drive and Kaiwi Street. The Kona Coast Shopping Center has a small Japanese bakery that opens Tuesday through Saturday with fresh anpan and melon pan.

For European-Style Pastry

That's us — Lehua Bake House. We're the place on the Kona Coast for butter croissants, proper laminates, and French pastry technique. Kalani trained in Paris specifically to bring this level of craft to Kona. We also believe strongly in Hawaiian ingredients and flavors, so you'll find guava, lilikoi, and haupia woven throughout the menu.

The Farmer's Market Circuit

For the best sampling experience, hit the Kailua-Kona Farmers Market on Wednesday and Saturday mornings near the Ali'i Drive waterfront. Multiple small bakers set up with everything from gluten-free banana bread to traditional Hawaiian sweet rolls. It's a great way to discover bakers who don't have storefronts.
